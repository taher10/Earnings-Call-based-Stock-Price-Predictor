Earnings Call AnalysisQ3-2023 AnalysisFord Motor CoMixed Performance and Strategic AdjustmentsAmidst a challenging year, we maintained a robust adjusted EBIT run rate, reaching $9.4 billion through Q3 and poised to hit $11-12 billion annual guidance; however, the UAW strike led us to withdraw this outlook due to uncertainties. The strike cost us roughly $1.3 billion in EBIT and affected 80,000 units. Our diversified portfolio showcased resilience with Ford Pro generating a substantial $1.7 billion EBIT for Q3 and Ford Blue up by $300 million, positively influenced by lower commodity costs. We've seen strong demand and pricing power, especially in robust franchises like Super Duty and Transit. Model e incurs a $1.3 billion loss but reflects necessary growth investments, with strategic cuts such as delaying a key battery plant to match production with market demand. Ford Credit faced a downtrend due to lower residuals and normalized credit loss performance. We're bolstering our software and service subscriptions, delivering around 50% gross margins. Our solid liquidity of over $29 billion ensures we are equipped to navigate current market volatility and invest in our transformative Ford+ plan.Commercial Strength and Strategic AdjustmentsAmid a backdrop of infrastructure projects and loyal customers, the commercial side of the business is buzzing with activity. Fleet orders for the 2024 Super Duty are eclipsing those from the previous year, with anticipation high for continued robust demand. In addition, we look towards the launch of the Transit Custom in Europe, a lynchpin in our European profit strategy and a top-selling vehicle. A key focus remains Ford Pro, our software and services division, which aims to increase productivity for business owners through data insights, vehicle control, and an expanded aftersales service business that promises much potential for growth. Additionally, our strategic turnaround in China is paying dividends, as the region is becoming profitable and an export hub to markets such as Mexico and South America. Competition is fierce, but our targeted approach in specific vehicle segments and partnerships is expected to bear fruit.Financial Resilience and Withdrawn GuidanceThe first three quarters of the year were significant, with $9.4 billion in adjusted EBIT, positioning us to comfortably meet our full-year adjusted EBIT guidance of $11 billion to $12 billion. However, the unpredictability introduced by the UAW strike has led us to withdraw this guidance, despite reaching a tentative agreement. The strike's impact, coupled with ongoing industry disruptions and resultant operational delays, creates significant uncertainties, one of which is an estimated $1.3 billion reduction in 2023 EBIT due to around 80,000 fewer units produced.Strong Liquidity and Operational HighlightsOur financial strength is evident with over $29 billion in cash, $50 billion in liquidity, and the addition of a $4 billion contingency to our strong quarter. Operational success within Ford Pro is notable, with $1.7 billion in EBIT and a double-digit margin of 12%, driven primarily by our Super Duty and Transit franchises. Despite flat whole sales affected by supply constraints and a UAW work stoppage, our revenue rose 11% thanks to a compelling product lineup and resilient net pricing.EV Strategy and Adjustments Amid LossesOur EV start-up, Model e, faced a $1.3 billion loss this quarter, reflecting ongoing investments in next-generation products and a more challenging market for current offerings. In response to the dynamic EV market, we're being cautious with production and investment, postponing plant openings and adjusting capacity to align with demand. Our Gen 2 and Gen 3 EV products are anticipated to be more cost-effective and benefit from the learnings of our current models. In contrast, the 'Ford Blue' business segment turned a profit with $1.7 billion in EBIT, thanks to lower commodity costs and higher net pricing, although higher warranty costs did challenge margins.Focus on Software and Services for GrowthA vital growth area for us lies within software and services, boasting a 50% gross margin and consistent quarter-over-quarter subscription growth across all segments. The upcoming next-generation digital platform is set to revolutionize our delivery capabilities, offering substantial value additions to both retail and commercial customers. We remain optimistic about the Ford+ plan and our business's underlying run rate, set to harness the transformative power of our digital endeavors.
