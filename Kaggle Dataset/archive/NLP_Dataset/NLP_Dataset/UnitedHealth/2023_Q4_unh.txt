Optum and UnitedHealthcare divisions both experienced double-digit growth. The company anticipates recording a one-time charge of approximately $7 billion due to the sale of its Brazil operations, which will not affect the 2024 adjusted earnings per share. Optum Health's revenues grew by 34% to over $95 billion, while OptumInsight and Optum Rx also saw significant growth, with revenues increasing to $18.9 billion and over $116 billion, respectively. The company expects a care ratio of 84% (±0.5%) and generated a strong cash flow of $29 billion in 2023, returning nearly $15 billion to shareholders through repurchases and dividends.Robust Revenue Growth Across the BoardThe company witnessed a revenue increase to $372 billion in 2023, marking a substantial 14% growth compared to the previous year. This impressive upturn was fueled by significant contributions from both healthcare services provider Optum and health insurance arm UnitedHealthcare. The year culminated with a fourth quarter adjusted earnings per share of $6.16, a 15% incline, thereby wrapping up the year with a full adjusted earnings per share of $25.12, translating to a 13% growth.Strategic Divestiture and Year-End OutlookStrategically streamlining its operations, the company is set to sell its Brazil business segment, expecting to finalize the deal in the first half of the upcoming year. This move will lead to a one-off charge of roughly $7 billion, primarily non-cash and reflection of cumulative foreign currency translation losses. Notably, this charge will not affect the adjusted earnings per share metric for 2024. Based on the last data, the company's revenue includes approximately $6 billion, or 1.5% of its consolidated revenue, from the Brazil operations. Even with these changes, the company remains optimistic about maintaining consistent care patterns, especially in outpatient services for the senior demographic, validating their benefit design approach and supporting a care ratio outlook of 84%, with an allowance of fluctuation by 50 basis points.Optum Subsidiaries Drive Spectacular GrowthOptumInsight, tied to data, analytics, and technology-enabled services, expanded its annual revenues by 30% to $18.9 billion, ending the year with a $32.1 billion revenue backlog, a $2.1 billion increase from the previous year. Such robust performance is ascribed to its diversified and ever-evolving product suite catering to various healthcare stakeholders—a trend which portends further growth for the company. The pharmacy care services provider, Optum Rx, also didn't lag, reporting a 16% jump in revenues topping $116 billion due to new client acquisitions, strengthened existing customer relationships, and organic expansion.UnitedHealthcare's Steady Rise and Forward-Looking ProjectionsUnitedHealthcare followed suit with over $281 billion in full year revenues, an almost 13% spike. The company foresees most of the growth in Medicare Advantage happening outside the annual enrollment period, reflecting recent patterns. Furthermore, the Medicaid segment projects a balanced enrollment trajectory in 2024, attributing to the completion of state redeterminations and organic growth within current states like North Carolina providing necessary mitigation against enrollment shifts.Company Exudes Confidence for 2024The company's performance in 2023 and the onset of the new year embolden its faith in meeting both 2024 and long-term growth goals previously communicated. With a well-fitted strategic compass and persistent efforts toward innovation and client satisfaction, the company anticipates sustained delivery against its set targets. Heading into 2024, management conveys a palpable confidence backed by a steadfast team aimed at constructing a more straightforward, customer-centric healthcare domain.
